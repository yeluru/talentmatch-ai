# TalentMatch AI - User Roles Guide

## What This Document Is For

This guide explains what each type of user can do in the TalentMatch AI system. If you're a candidate, recruiter, manager, or admin, read your section to understand all the features available to you. No technical jargon‚Äîjust straightforward explanations of what you can accomplish.

## üéØ Candidate

**Who you are:** A job seeker looking for opportunities and tracking your applications.

**What you can do:**

### Your Profile & Information
- Create and update your profile with your name, contact details, location, and professional summary
- Upload your resume (PDF or Word document) so recruiters can review your experience
- Add your work history, including job titles, companies, dates, and descriptions of what you did
- List your education, including degrees, schools, and graduation years
- Highlight your skills and technologies you know (e.g., Python, Project Management, Sales)
- Add certifications and professional credentials you've earned
- Provide links to your LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio, or personal website

### Finding & Applying to Jobs
- Browse all open job positions posted by recruiters in the system
- Search for jobs by title, keywords, location, or job type (full-time, contract, remote, etc.)
- Filter jobs by category, experience level, or salary range
- Read full job descriptions including responsibilities, requirements, and company information
- Apply to jobs with one click using your profile information
- See which jobs you've already applied to so you don't apply twice
- Withdraw your application if you change your mind or accept another offer

### Tracking Your Applications
- View a dashboard showing all your active applications in one place
- See what stage each application is at (Applied, Screening, Interview, Offer, etc.)
- Know which recruiter is handling your application for each job
- Track when you applied and when your status last changed
- Get notified when your application status changes (moved to interview, offered, etc.)
- See a timeline of all actions taken on your applications

### Communication & Updates
- Receive email notifications when recruiters contact you or update your status
- Respond to messages from recruiters about your applications
- Get alerts when new jobs matching your profile are posted
- See notes or feedback recruiters have shared with you about your application
- Upload additional documents if a recruiter requests them (references, certifications, etc.)

### Managing Your Job Search
- Set your job search status (actively looking, open to opportunities, not looking)
- Indicate your preferred job types, locations, and salary expectations
- Track which companies and roles you're most interested in
- Save jobs you want to apply to later
- See statistics on your applications (how many submitted, response rate, etc.)

## üíº Recruiter

**Who you are:** Someone who sources candidates, manages the hiring pipeline, and works to fill open positions.

**What you can do:**

### Managing Your Jobs
- Create new job postings with title, description, requirements, and salary information
- Edit or update job details anytime (close applications, change requirements, update salary)
- Mark jobs as Open or Closed depending on hiring status
- Link jobs to specific clients if you work with multiple companies
- See how many applications each of your jobs has received
- Track which jobs are performing well (lots of applications) vs. need more sourcing
- Archive old jobs to keep your workspace clean

### Sourcing & Finding Candidates
- Access a shared Talent Pool of all candidates in your organization
- Search the talent pool by name, skills, experience, location, or keywords
- Filter candidates by their current status (New, Contacted, Screening, etc.)
- View candidate profiles showing resume, work history, education, and skills
- Import candidates in bulk by uploading a CSV file with contact information
- Add individual candidates manually if you found them on LinkedIn or elsewhere
- Tag candidates with labels to organize them (e.g., "Senior Dev", "Marketing Expert")

### Engaging with Candidates
- Engage candidates to a specific job by adding them to that job's pipeline
- Move candidates through your pipeline stages: Engaged ‚Üí Applied ‚Üí RTR & Rate ‚Üí Doc Check ‚Üí Screening ‚Üí Submission ‚Üí Outcome
- Disengage candidates from a job if they're no longer a fit (moves them back to Talent Pool)
- Send Right to Represent (RTR) emails to candidates with rate and job details
- Request documents from candidates (ID, certifications, references)
- Record notes about conversations, interviews, or candidate feedback
- Mark candidates with outcomes: Client Rejected, Job Offered, Candidate Declined, Withdrawn, Hired

### Pipeline & Application Management
- View all your active candidates organized by job and pipeline stage
- See at a glance how many candidates are in each stage (visual funnel)
- Move candidates forward or backward through stages with one click
- Set candidate status and track their progress through the hiring process
- Add internal notes that only your team can see (candidate strengths, interview feedback, concerns)
- Schedule reminders to follow up with candidates at specific dates
- Bulk update multiple candidates at once (move 5 people to Screening stage together)

### Communication & Outreach
- Send emails directly to candidates from within the system
- Use email templates for common messages (interview invites, rejection letters, follow-ups)
- Track email open rates and candidate engagement
- Create outreach campaigns to contact multiple candidates about a job opportunity
- Send personalized messages with job details and why you think they're a good fit
- Record phone call notes and conversation summaries
- Set up automatic follow-up reminders if candidates don't respond

### Client & Job Details
- Link your jobs to specific clients so you can track which client each position is for
- View client contact information and account manager assigned to each client
- See all jobs you're working on for a particular client
- Track submission counts and hiring outcomes per client for reporting

### Reporting & Analytics
- View your personal dashboard showing your jobs, applications, and pipeline metrics
- See how many candidates you've sourced, engaged, and submitted this week/month
- Track conversion rates (how many candidates move from stage to stage)
- Review your activity timeline showing what you did each day
- Export candidate lists or application reports for your records
- Monitor your performance: applications per job, time-to-fill, submission success rate

### Team Collaboration
- See other recruiters' candidates in the shared Talent Pool
- Avoid duplicate outreach by checking if someone else already contacted a candidate
- Hand off candidates to other recruiters if job ownership changes
- Tag candidates for other team members to review
- Share notes and feedback about candidates with your team

## üìä Manager / Account Manager

**Who you are:** Someone who oversees recruiters, monitors organization performance, and ensures the team hits hiring goals.

**What you can do:**

### Organization Dashboard & Overview
- View a high-level dashboard showing all recruiting activity across your entire organization
- See total open jobs, total applications, active candidates, and team size at a glance
- Monitor the pipeline funnel to see how many candidates are at each stage org-wide
- Track application trends over the last 30 days with charts and percentage changes
- Identify jobs or candidates that need immediate attention (stalled applications, pending actions)
- Access quick links to jump directly to Team, Jobs, Analytics, or Audit Logs

### Team Management & Oversight
- View a complete list of all recruiters and account managers in your organization
- See each team member's name, email, role, and activity metrics
- Invite new recruiters or account managers by sending them an email signup link
- Assign roles to team members (Recruiter, Account Manager, Org Admin)
- Deactivate team members who leave the organization
- Assign Account Managers to oversee specific recruiters for coaching and accountability
- Search and filter the team list to find specific people quickly

### Recruiter Performance Tracking
- View detailed performance metrics for any individual recruiter
- See how many jobs each recruiter owns and how many applications they have
- Track how many candidates a recruiter has added to the talent pool
- Review pipeline distribution (how many candidates each recruiter has at each stage)
- Check when a recruiter last took action to ensure they're staying active
- Monitor sourcing effectiveness (where recruiters are finding candidates)
- Compare recruiter performance to identify top performers and those who need help

### "View As" Recruiter Mode
- Switch to see the system exactly as a specific recruiter sees it
- View that recruiter's jobs, pipeline, candidates, and outreach campaigns
- See which candidates they're working on and what stage those candidates are in
- Review their activity timeline and recent actions
- Use this view for coaching sessions or auditing compliance
- Switch back to manager view anytime to regain org-wide visibility

### Jobs Overview & Management
- See all jobs across the entire organization, regardless of which recruiter owns them
- Filter jobs by recruiter, status (open/closed), client, or date created
- Sort jobs by application count to see which positions are most/least active
- Click on any job to see full details, applicants, and pipeline metrics
- Reassign jobs to different recruiters if workload needs balancing
- Identify jobs with zero applications that need more attention or sourcing efforts
- Export the jobs list for reporting to leadership or clients

### Candidate Visibility Across Recruiters
- View all candidates linked to your organization, across all recruiters
- Filter candidates by job, recruiter, status, or date
- See which candidates are being considered by multiple recruiters
- Review candidate profiles, applications, and timelines from an org-wide perspective
- Identify candidates who may have fallen through the cracks or need follow-up
- Export candidate lists for cross-recruiter reporting or analysis

### Client Account Management
- View all clients your organization serves (hiring companies or departments)
- Create new client accounts with contact information and details
- Assign Account Managers or recruiters to specific client accounts
- Link jobs to clients for organized reporting and tracking
- See all jobs associated with a particular client in one view
- Track how many positions you're filling for each client and the status of those hires

### Team Activity Feed
- See a real-time feed of all actions taken by your team (who engaged a candidate, who moved someone to Screening, who created a job)
- Filter activity by recruiter to focus on one person's work
- Filter by action type (only see engagements, only see status changes)
- Filter by date range (today, last 7 days, custom range)
- Use the feed for daily stand-ups, team meetings, or quick status checks
- Spot trends (e.g., lots of submissions this week) or identify inactivity

### Analytics & Reporting
- View pipeline conversion rates (how many candidates move from one stage to the next)
- See application trends over time with visual charts
- Track recruiter activity and productivity across the team
- Identify bottlenecks (stages where candidates get stuck)
- Review sourcing effectiveness (which channels bring the best candidates)
- Export reports for leadership presentations or client meetings

### Audit Logs & Compliance
- Access a complete history of all actions taken in the system (who did what, when)
- Filter audit logs by date range, user, action type, or specific job/candidate
- See before/after values for changes (status changed from Screening to Submission)
- Use audit logs for compliance, troubleshooting, or understanding system usage
- Export audit logs for external audits or record-keeping
- Review logs to answer questions like "Who changed this candidate's status?" or "When was this job created?"

### Organization Settings
- Update organization name and branding (logo, theme)
- Create and manage invite codes for recruiters or candidates to join
- See usage statistics for invite codes (how many people signed up with each code)
- Configure org-wide settings like time zone or notification preferences
- Manage organization-level data that affects the entire team

### Role Switching (Account Managers)
- Switch between Manager role (org-wide oversight) and Recruiter role (your own recruiting work)
- Use Manager role to see the whole organization, oversee team, and access analytics
- Switch to Recruiter role to do hands-on recruiting: your jobs, your pipeline, your candidates
- The Talent Pool stays shared across both roles
- Switch back and forth anytime depending on what you need to do

## üîß Organization Admin

**Who you are:** The person responsible for setting up and managing your organization's TalentMatch AI account.

**What you can do:**

### Everything Managers Can Do
- All the features listed in the Manager/Account Manager section above
- Full visibility into all recruiting activity, team performance, and organization data

### Advanced Team Management
- Create user accounts for anyone in your organization
- Assign or change user roles (Recruiter, Account Manager, Org Admin)
- Deactivate or reactivate user accounts
- Reset passwords for team members who are locked out
- Manage user permissions and access levels
- View detailed user activity logs for security and compliance

### Organization Configuration
- Set up your organization's structure (departments, teams, regions)
- Configure system-wide settings and defaults
- Customize pipeline stages if your organization uses different stages than the default
- Set up custom fields or data points your organization tracks
- Configure email templates and branding for candidate communications
- Manage integrations with other tools your organization uses

### Data Management
- Import large batches of historical data (old candidates, past jobs)
- Export organization data for backups or migration
- Clean up duplicate records or merge candidate profiles
- Archive old data to keep the system running smoothly
- Manage data retention policies for compliance

### Billing & Subscription
- View your organization's subscription plan and usage
- Upgrade or downgrade your plan based on team size or needs
- Manage payment methods and billing information
- See invoices and payment history
- Add or remove user seats as your team grows or shrinks

### Security & Compliance
- Review security settings and access controls
- Set up two-factor authentication requirements for users
- Configure data privacy settings to comply with regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Review and approve sensitive actions like bulk deletes
- Manage API access and third-party integrations

## Getting Started by Role

### If you're a Candidate:
1. Complete your profile with resume, work history, and skills
2. Browse open jobs and apply to positions that interest you
3. Track your applications on your dashboard
4. Respond promptly when recruiters contact you

### If you're a Recruiter:
1. Create your first job posting or review existing jobs assigned to you
2. Explore the Talent Pool to find candidates who match your jobs
3. Engage candidates to your jobs and move them through the pipeline
4. Use email templates and outreach campaigns to contact multiple candidates efficiently

### If you're a Manager/Account Manager:
1. Review the organization dashboard to understand current recruiting activity
2. Check in on your team's performance using Team Activity and recruiter progress pages
3. Identify jobs that need attention (low applications, stalled pipelines)
4. Use "view as" mode to coach recruiters on specific candidates or strategies

### If you're an Org Admin:
1. Set up your organization settings, branding, and invite codes
2. Invite your team members and assign appropriate roles
3. Configure pipeline stages and system settings to match your processes
4. Monitor usage and ensure team members are using the system effectively

### If you're a Super Admin:
1. Monitor platform health and performance across all organizations
2. Provide support to organization admins when they encounter issues
3. Review platform-wide usage trends and identify opportunities for improvement
4. Manage system updates, feature deployments, and maintenance windows
